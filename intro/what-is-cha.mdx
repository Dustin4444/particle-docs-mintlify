**Chain abstraction** removes the friction of dealing with multiple blockchains.
With **Universal Accounts**, users and developers interact with Web3 as if it were one unified environment.

---

## What this means for users

- **One account and balance** across all chains — assets everywhere are automatically combined.
- **Use any token as gas** on any transaction.
- **No bridges or chain switching** required.

---

## What this means for developers

- Build your dApp on one chain, serve users from all of them.
- No need to manage cross-chain deployments or per-chain liquidity.
- Cross-chain complexity is handled automatically in the background.

---

## How it works under the hood: Universal Liquidity

**Universal Liquidity** is the mechanism that makes chain abstraction possible. It lets Universal Accounts fulfill user actions on any chain without requiring users to bridge or pre-position assets.

When a user performs an action on a chain where they don't hold funds, Universal Liquidity handles the cross-chain complexity behind the scenes using **solvers** — on-chain entities with liquidity across supported networks.

**Flow:**

1. The user initiates an action (swap, mint, deposit, etc.) and signs once to authorize it. No preparation of assets or gas on the target chain is required.
2. The signature conveys what the user wants to do and confirms they can cover the cost.
3. Solvers detect the request and temporarily provide the required liquidity on the destination chain. They execute the transaction on the user's behalf, cover costs upfront, then recoup repayment from the user's balances on any chain.
4. The transaction finalizes on the destination network.

The user's funds never move beforehand. The solver fronts the required liquidity, then withdraws repayment from existing balances according to the signed authorization.

**Example:** A user holds 100 USDC on Base, 0.1 BNB on BNB Chain, and 0.3 SOL on Solana. They want to execute a transaction requiring 200 USDC on Ethereum. A solver provides the 200 USDC on Ethereum, then receives repayment across the user's existing balances plus a small fee — all from a single signature.

---

## Gas abstraction

Universal Liquidity also removes the requirement to hold native gas tokens on every chain. Paymasters cover gas fees on the user's behalf, then settle repayment using any supported asset — even if that asset is on another chain.

**Flow:**

1. User signs to authorize the operation and repayment.
2. Paymaster fronts the gas immediately.
3. Transaction executes on the target chain.
4. Repayment is deducted afterward using available balances.

A single signature is enough to complete cross-chain operations while advancing both liquidity and gas.

---

## TL;DR

Universal Liquidity enables:

- Assets across chains to function as **one unified liquidity source**
- Execution of transactions using **any combination** of a user's balances
- Removal of **bridging**, **gas concerns**, and **balance fragmentation**
